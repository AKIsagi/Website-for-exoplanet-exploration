UNDERSTANDING EXOPLANETS

      Exoplanets is a word which originates from the word “planet” and “exo” which means “world” and “outsider” combined
      The planets which are outside the solar system and orbit other stars or are rogue worlds are known as exoplanets.
      Such undesirably vivid worlds have many different environments far superior or weirder than the realms of the solar system.

THE TRANSIT METHOD:

      The transit method is one of the most used methods to find exoplanets. This method involves watching the host star of the planet closely through a telescope. If the star blinks or emits lesser light than expected, it can be taken into consideration that a celestial body is in its orbit. Sometimes, smaller spots are also observed which are also considered. If such activity takes place after regular intervals, then it proves that a celestial body is in the star’s orbit. Thus, the size and gravitational force of the planet is also calculated.
